Why it counts as evidence

Built like a flight recorder, not like a log file

Chained entries

Every entry is cryptographically locked to the one before it. Edit, delete, insert, or reorder anything and the chain visibly breaks at that exact point.

Signed record

The recording is signed (Ed25519). A forger without the key can't re-seal the file — the record is attributable, not just intact.

Crash-proof checkpoints

The recorder seals a checkpoint every few seconds of activity. Even if the machine dies mid-flight, everything up to the last checkpoint stays provable.

No vendor lock — ever

Traces use ATF, an open, royalty-free format. Anyone can verify a recording with ~100 lines of standard cryptography. Evidence that only we could read would be worthless to you.

Stated limits

What we don't record — said out loud

Compliance runs on precision, so here is our coverage boundary, plainly:

  • Unwrapped connections aren't recorded. Each tool connection you wrap is covered; a door without a camera isn't filmed. Wrapping all of them takes seconds apiece.
  • Other companies' AIs are their problem. We record your side of every interaction — what your agent did and was asked. Like dashcams, each party carries its own recorder.
  • We prove the record wasn't altered afterwards — not that the machine it ran on was uncompromised at recording time. Full threat model ships with the product.

Questions buyers actually ask

Straight answers

What exactly is a "flight"?

One work session of your agent, start to finish — one refund handled, one claim processed. The name comes from aviation's black box, which records one flight the same way. Replay shows that sequence step by step.

Will it slow down or change our agent?

No. The adapter relays every message byte-for-byte untouched and seals copies out-of-band — microseconds per event. A recorder that altered behavior would be worthless as evidence; transparency is a design invariant.

We already log everything. Why isn't that enough?

Logs prove what your database says now — not that nobody changed it. Protokoll's chained, signed record makes any later alteration mathematically detectable. That's the difference between a log and evidence.

What about sensitive data in the recordings?

You define redaction rules (emails, IBANs, names — any pattern). Matches are stripped before anything touches disk; the seal still proves what the original content was, without containing it. Built for GDPR data-minimization from day one.

Are we locked into you?

No. The trace format (ATF) is an open, royalty-free specification, and verification is standard cryptography anyone can implement. Your evidence outlives any vendor — including us.

Section 04Evidence design

Built like a flight recorder, not a log file

ChainedSealEvery entry is cryptographically locked to the one before it. Edit, delete, insert, or reorder anything — the chain breaks visibly at that exact point.
SignedEd25519The record is signed. A forger without the key cannot re-seal the file: the recording is attributable, not merely intact.
Crash-proofCheckpointThe recorder seals checkpoints as it runs. If the machine dies mid-flight, everything up to the last checkpoint remains provable.
Open formatATFTraces use an open, royalty-free format; verification is ~100 lines of standard cryptography anyone can implement. Evidence only we could read would be worthless to you.

Section 07Inquiries

Questions buyers actually ask

INQ-01What exactly is a "flight"?

One work session of your agent, start to finish — one refund handled, one claim processed. The name comes from aviation's recorder, which documents one flight the same way. Replay shows that sequence step by step.

INQ-02Will it slow down or change our agent?

No. The adapter relays every message untouched and seals copies out-of-band — microseconds per event. Transparency is a design invariant: a recorder that altered behavior couldn't be trusted as evidence.

INQ-03We already log everything. Why isn't that enough?

Logs prove what your database says now — not that nobody changed it. A chained, signed record makes any later alteration mathematically detectable. That is the difference between a log and evidence.

INQ-04What about other AIs interacting with ours?

Everything crossing your wrapped connections is recorded — including what outside agents asked yours to do. What other companies' AIs do on their own systems is their liability and their recorder's job, exactly like dashcams in traffic.

INQ-05Are we locked in?

No. The trace format (ATF) is an open, royalty-free specification; anyone can verify a recording independently, forever. Your evidence outlives every vendor — including us.
